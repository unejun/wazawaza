Cookie Policy

Last Updated: 2026-06-10  |  Effective Date: 2026-06-10

This Cookie Policy (the "Policy") explains how MN Inc. ("we", "us", or "the Company") uses cookies and similar technologies on the official wazawaza project website (the "Site"). This Policy forms part of, and should be read together with, our separate Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device through your browser when you visit a website. They may be "first-party cookies" (set by us) or "third-party cookies" (set by other providers, such as embedded form services). In this Policy, the term "cookies" also includes similar technologies such as local storage, session storage, and pixel tags.

2. Cookies Used on this Site

The Site is an art project information page. We deliberately minimize commercial tracking. The cookies currently used on the Site are:

Category Purpose Provider Lifespan Consent
Strictly Necessary Basic site functionality (language, security) MN Inc. (first-party) Session up to 1 year Not required
Functional Remember language / region preference MN Inc. (first-party) Up to 1 year Required where applicable consent laws apply
Third-Party Embed Display and operation of the application and contact forms Third-party form service provider Per the form service provider's policy (up to 1 year) Required where applicable consent laws apply
Tracking Not Currently In Use.

The Site does not currently use Google Analytics, Google Tag Manager, Meta Pixel, X (Twitter) Pixel, TikTok Pixel, or any other advertising or behavioral-tracking tool. Should we adopt any such tools in the future, this Policy will be updated and, where required by applicable law, we will obtain consent or provide opt-out mechanisms in advance.

3. About the Third-Party Form Embeds

Both the offer-reception form and the general inquiry/contact form on the Site are provided by a third-party form service provider. The Company contracts with that provider under a plan that includes a Data Processing Agreement. In serving these forms, the third-party provider sets its own cookies and processes the user's IP address, browser information, and form responses. For details, see the third-party form service provider's own privacy and cookie notices.

4. Consent (Where Applicable Cookie Consent Laws Apply)

Where applicable cookie consent laws apply to the data subject, prior, freely-given, and informed consent is required before setting cookies that are not strictly necessary. We display a cookie consent banner and obtain consent where required by such laws. Consent may be withdrawn at any time through the same mechanism.

5. "Sale" / "Share" Opt-Out

The Company does not sell personal information. Where applicable privacy law grants a right to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information, the Company honors such requests submitted through the contact form on the Project's website. The Company also honors Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals sent by the user's browser.

6. How to Disable Cookies

Most browsers allow users to accept or reject cookies, delete previously-stored cookies, and configure related preferences. Please consult your browser's help documentation. Disabling strictly necessary cookies may impair the functioning of the Site.

  • Google Chrome — Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data.
  • Safari — Settings → Privacy → Manage Website Data.
  • Firefox — Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data.
  • Microsoft Edge — Settings → Cookies and site permissions.

7. "Do Not Track" Signals

There is no commonly agreed industry standard for responding to browser "Do Not Track" signals, and the Site does not currently respond to such signals automatically. We will revisit this position if and when an industry standard emerges.

8. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time to reflect technical changes or changes in law. Material changes will be announced on the Site and, where required by law, we will seek renewed consent.

9. Contact

Inquiries about this Policy or about Cookie handling may be made through the contact form on the Project's website.
