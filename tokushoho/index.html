The mode of transport (e.g., air or sea) and the delivery schedule are determined in the individual purchase agreement, taking into account the product, the destination, and operational factors at the time. Estimated delivery timeframes vary depending on the mode of transport and destination, and may range from several weeks to several months.

8. Returns, Exchanges, Cancellations, and Inspection Period

No Returns — Limited Edition Collector Work.

The Works form a worldwide limited edition of ten (10) collector art pieces, each individually serial-numbered. Each Buyer is allocated a specific serial number through the Seller's curated selection and acquires that piece. No returns, exchanges, cancellations, or price reductions at the Buyer's discretion will be accepted, whether before or after delivery.

The cooling-off provisions under the Japanese Act on Specified Commercial Transactions (Act No. 57 of 1976) do not apply to this form of mail-order sale.

To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, all sales are final. Where the Buyer's country of residence grants any non-waivable consumer right (such as a statutory right of withdrawal), the Buyer's statutory rights under that law are preserved, and the specific application is addressed in the individual purchase agreement entered into for that sale.

Inspection Period (via Escrow Service). Following confirmed delivery, the Buyer has a seven (7) day inspection period during which to either approve receipt or raise an objection through the escrow service's dispute procedure for evident transit damage or initial defect. If no valid objection is raised within seven (7) days, the escrow funds are released to the Seller and the transaction is finalized. Minor variations consistent with the handmade nature of the Work are not considered defects.
