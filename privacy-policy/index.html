2. Personal Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal data through (i) the offer-reception form, (ii) the general inquiry/contact form, (iii) post-selection contracting, and (iv) the escrow service provider designated by the Company for buyer identity verification:

  • Applicant identification data (offer-reception form): full name, country of residence, country and locality of intended delivery, organization name and representative's role (for organizational applicants).
  • Contact information: email address, optionally telephone number and social-media handles.
  • Offer-related information: offered price (USD), preferred edition number, optional message to the Seller.
  • Inquiry information (contact form): name, email address, subject, and message contents.
  • Identity verification data: collected by the escrow service provider designated by the Company directly from the Buyer following selection (e.g., government-issued identification, address verification, and source-of-funds where applicable). The Company does not collect or store original identity-verification documents directly; the Company receives verification status and limited identifiers from the escrow service provider only as necessary to perform the contract.
  • Contracting data: full legal name, complete delivery address, recipient details, and details necessary for export and customs declarations (collected after selection, for the purpose of the individual purchase agreement, electronic signature collection, and shipping).
  • Payment-related data: bank wire references and transaction identifiers as needed for reconciliation (the Seller does not collect bank account credentials).
  • Communications: emails, messages, support records, and case history.
  • Marketing consent records: where consent is given, we record the consent date, the consent text version, and the email address used for future communications.
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, device information, access logs, cookies (see the separate Cookie Policy).

3. Purposes of Processing

  1. Receipt and review of purchase offers submitted through the offer-reception form.
  2. Curated selection of Buyers from the received offers.
  3. Anti-Social Forces and basic sanctions screening conducted internally by the Company on selected candidates (using publicly available information and commercial databases) for risk-management purposes; comprehensive Buyer identity verification is conducted by the escrow service provider designated by the Company as part of the escrow transaction setup.
  4. Confirmation of compliance with export/import regulations of Japan and the destination country.
  5. Execution and performance of the individual purchase agreement, including signature collection via an electronic signature service.
  6. Manufacturing, packing, freight, insurance, customs clearance, and delivery.
  7. Escrow administration, payment reconciliation, and processing of any objections raised during the inspection period.
  8. Handling of inquiries received via the general inquiry/contact form.
  9. Marketing of future editions and related works, only where the data subject has provided separate, optional consent (see Article 4 below).
  10. Accounting, tax, and other statutory record-keeping.
  11. Responding to inquiries, claims, complaints, or legal proceedings, and otherwise defending the Company's legitimate interests.

4. Separation of Required Consents and Optional Marketing Consent

At the time of offer submission, the data subject is asked to confirm a series of short, topic-specific items (covering the non-binding nature of the offer, the curated selection process, additional charges, applicable taxes and customs duties, the identity verification process conducted via the escrow service provider, and acknowledgment of this Policy). These required confirmations are presented separately from any marketing-related consent.

Marketing consent is entirely optional, defaults to unselected, and is processed independently of the offer review. Refusing or withdrawing marketing consent does not affect the receipt or evaluation of the offer in any way. Marketing communications, if consented to, are limited to information about this project, related works by the Seller, and future editions; they will not be used for unrelated third-party promotions. Consent may be withdrawn at any time by following the unsubscribe instructions in each communication or by contacting the Privacy Contact.

5. Legal Basis (Where Applicable Data Protection Law Requires)

Where applicable data protection laws require us to specify a legal basis for processing, we process personal data on one or more of the following bases (categorized using terminology commonly recognized under such laws):

  • Performance of a contract — to conclude and perform the purchase agreement at the data subject's request.
  • Compliance with legal obligations — including KYC, AML, tax, customs, and export-control obligations.
  • Legitimate interests — fraud prevention, buyer selection, defense of legal claims, where such interests are not overridden by the data subject's rights and freedoms.
  • Consent — for marketing communications and other optional processing.

Where KYC documentation or related materials contain biometric or other data classified as "special" or "sensitive" categories of personal data under applicable law, processing is based on the data subject's explicit consent.

8. Retention Period

  • Offer-reception data (applicants not selected) and contact-form inquiry data: retained only for the period reasonably necessary for review, response, and recordkeeping, unless retention is required by law or for ongoing matters.
  • Purchase agreement and related records: retained for the period required to perform the contract, satisfy applicable limitation periods, and comply with applicable laws.
  • Tax, accounting, and export-related records: retained for the period required under applicable law.
  • Marketing consent records: retained for the duration of the active subscription and for a reasonable evidentiary period following withdrawal of consent.
  • Buyer identity verification: performed and retained by the escrow service provider under its own retention policies; the Company retains only minimal identifiers necessary to perform the contract.

Upon expiry of the applicable retention period, data is securely deleted or anonymized.
